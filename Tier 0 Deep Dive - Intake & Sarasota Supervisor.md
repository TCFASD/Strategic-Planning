## The intake question — the central Tier 0 decision

Initial thinking: leave intake split because consolidating would add to Sarasota Supervisor's already heavy load.

Revised thinking: **do intake supervisory consolidation in Tier 0** — and here's the reasoning.

### Why intake consolidation in Tier 0 actually works

In Tier 0, "consolidation" doesn't require a new hire. It's just a supervision change — the virtual Intake Coordinator moves from Orlando supervision to Sarasota Site Director supervision. Same FTE, same work, same person, just a different supervisor.

| Sarasota Site Director | Today | Tier 0 (without intake consolidation) | Tier 0 (with intake consolidation) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reports | 3 | 2 (lost PA) | 3 (lost PA, gained Virtual Intake) |
| Composition | MH Clinician, Sarasota Intake, PA | MH Clinician, Sarasota Intake | **MH Clinician, Sarasota Intake, Virtual Intake** |

She ends up with the **same number of reports** as today — but a much more coherent portfolio: clinical (1) + intake (2). That's a story she can tell about her role.

### Why this might be the right move

1. **It addresses what she's actually asking for.** She wanted intake under her. Tier 0 gives her supervisory ownership even though full implementation waits for new hires. She begins designing what "intake as main landing place" looks like under her leadership now.
2. **The workload math actually works.** Both Parent Advocate and Virtual Intake Coordinator are virtual roles. Supervision overhead is similar. She's not adding meaningful weight, just swapping one virtual report for another.
3. **Makes the new Orlando hire's onboarding more sustainable.** Without intake consolidation, new Orlando hire has 4 reports. With consolidation: 3 reports. Much more humane for someone brand new.
4. **Cleaner portfolios all around.**
   - Sarasota: Clinic ops + Clinical + Intake (consistent thread)
   - Orlando: Family services + Interns + Panhandle Clinic Lead
   - The new hire's portfolio matches her strengths better.
5. **Moves intake-as-landing-place forward strategically even without new hires.** Even with only 2 intake people, having one named owner means the work can begin: shared protocols, joint meetings, family-experience design, prep for what the team becomes when funded.

### What this still doesn't solve

- **Clinic ops is still on her plate.** That's the bigger workload issue and intake consolidation doesn't touch it. May need its own conversation eventually — possibly a Clinic Operations Coordinator role in a future tier, or restructuring her role to be primarily clinic ops.
- **True intake transformation needs Tier 1+.** Two people, however well supervised, can only do so much.
- **The recognition need probably needs more than title + intake ownership.** Visibility (presents at funder meetings? leadership?), agency materials (named as operational lead?), compensation if there's any room.

## Recommended Tier 0 design

1. **Title shift** to Sarasota Site Director (or alternative).
2. **Both virtual staff change supervisors on July 1** — PA moves to Orlando, Virtual Intake moves to Sarasota. Clean swap.
3. **Sarasota Site Director is named "Intake Lead"** (or similar) in addition to her supervisory title — formalizes the strategic ownership.
4. **The 1:1 conversation** acknowledges the load AND frames intake ownership as her path to grow into the role, not a consolation prize.
5. **Hold off on adding the Coordinator's regional outreach work to her plate** — not where her strength is and would dilute clinic ops focus.

## Transition risk: two simultaneous supervisor changes

If both PA AND Virtual Intake change supervisors on July 1, that's two transitions for two staff at once, in the same week the new Orlando hire is settling in.

Options:
- **Stagger:** PA moves July 1, Virtual Intake moves October 1 (or vice versa). Drags transition out for 6 months and creates ongoing ambiguity.
- **Both on July 1** with explicit Tamra check-ins for both staff in the first 60 days.
- **Both on July 1** with warm handoffs (joint meetings between old and new supervisor for the first month).

**Lean:** Both on July 1 with warm handoffs + Tamra check-ins.
