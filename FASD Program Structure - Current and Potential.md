# FASD Program Structure — Current and Potential

*Date: April 17, 2026*
*Reference document. Captures current organizational structure and potential expansion if additional funding is secured.*

---

## Current Structure

**Tamra (FASD Director)** directly supervises:
- Sarasota Supervisor
- Orlando Supervisor
- Outreach & Engagement Coordinator (non-supervisory)

### Sarasota Supervisor supervises:
- Mental Health Clinician (Sarasota)
- Intake Coordinator (Sarasota — in-person intake)
- Parent Advocate (virtual)

### Orlando Supervisor supervises:
- Clinic Lead (Panhandle)
- Intake Coordinator (virtual)
- Student Interns (MH intern program)

### Notes on current structure
- Supervision is split by both **location** and **program type** (e.g., one supervisor owns in-person intake, the other owns virtual intake — both end up touching the same program).
- Some staff are virtual but assigned to a location-based supervisor (e.g., virtual Parent Advocate under Sarasota, virtual Intake Coordinator under Orlando).
- The Panhandle Clinic Lead reports to the Orlando Supervisor.

## Potential Future Structure (if funded)

### Role changes
- **Move virtual Intake Coordinator → Parent Advocate role** (expanded scope: caregiver support + educational navigation)
- **Hire new Intake Coordinator(s)** — one full-time, possibly one part-time
- **Goal:** intake department becomes the main landing place for *all* families

### Intake consolidation question (open)
The Sarasota Supervisor has expressed interest in consolidating **all intake in Sarasota** under her supervision.
- *Her case:* easier for training, accountability, and team cohesion when intake is one team in one place.
- *Tamra's concern:* this would significantly grow her span of control. Need to weigh operational benefit against supervisor capacity and the dynamic that gave rise to the earlier "threatened supervisor" thread.

### New positions if funded
- **Regional Outreach & Engagement staff:**
  - One in Orlando
  - One in Miami (expansion territory)
- **Clinic Lead in Miami** — possible with increased funding
- **Second Mental Health Clinician closer to Orlando** — if the wait list grows enough to warrant it

### Implications worth flagging
1. **The Outreach & Engagement Coordinator role evolves substantially.** With regional outreach staff in Orlando and Miami, she may shift from non-supervisory to supervisor of a regional outreach team. That changes the leadership-meeting question entirely — at that point she *would* be a supervisor and the structural reason for excluding her dissolves.
2. **Miami creates a third location**, which adds another layer to the location-vs-program supervision question. Worth resolving the existing ambiguity *before* expansion makes it more complex.
3. **Intake consolidation has a tradeoff:** simplifies one part of the structure (intake = one team, one supervisor), complicates another (one supervisor with a much wider span). May also be worth considering whether intake-consolidation creates a natural moment to clarify the location-vs-program supervision lines for the rest of the program.
4. **The MH clinician geography:** if a second clinician is hired closer to Orlando, then clinical staff are split across sites — supervision design has to account for this.
